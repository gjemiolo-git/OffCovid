If you did not know, a PCR test is the one you take if you have symptoms or if:

  • you’ve been in contact with someone who’s tested positive
  • you’ve been asked to get a test by a local council or someone from NHS Test and Trace
  • a GP or other health professional has asked you to get a test
  • you’re taking part in a government pilot project
  • you’ve been asked to get a test to confirm a positive result
  • you’ve received an unclear result and were told to get a second test
  • you need to get a test for someone you live with who has symptoms
  • you’re in the National Tactical Response Group



To do a PCR test you will need to book a PCR test appointement by clicking here.
Whilst if you don't have symptoms or you need a test to travel you should book a lateral flow test (see the Lateral Flow Test Information section).
